Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Sustainability

Infrastructure

Infrastructure comprises essential facilities and systems enabling societal and growth.

Empowerment

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a renowned Bangladeshi economist, social entrepreneur, and the founder of Grameen Bank. He is the pioneer of the concept of microcredit, which has brought transformative change in poverty alleviation. In 1983, he established Grameen Bank to provide microloans to poor individuals who were excluded from traditional financial services. Dr. Yunus and Grameen Bank jointly received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. He is also a leading advocate of "social business," focusing on sustainable solutions to global issues. Dr. Yunus remains a symbol of human development and financial inclusion worldwide."
